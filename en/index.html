The complete system

Yoga has six paths — most studios teach only one

The classical tradition recognises six major paths of yoga. Each addresses a different dimension of the human being. A complete practice draws from all of them. Here is what each one offers — and how I weave them into your sessions.

🔥
Karma Yoga

The Path of Action

Acting without attachment to results. Builds inner freedom, reduces reactivity, and grounds the practice in daily life — not just on the mat.

💡
Jnana Yoga

The Path of Knowledge

Self-inquiry, philosophical understanding, discriminative awareness. Helps you see through mental patterns and understand the nature of the mind.

🙏
Bhakti Yoga

The Path of Devotion

Channelling emotion into practice — gratitude, openness, surrender. Transforms emotional energy from a source of suffering into a source of strength.

🌿
Hatha Yoga

The Path of the Body

Asana, pranayama, mudra, bandha — systematic practices to purify and awaken the physical and energetic body. This is the foundation of most sessions.

⚡
Ashtanga (Raja) Yoga

The Path of the Mind

Patanjali's eight-limbed system: ethical living, sensory withdrawal, concentration, meditation. A structured map for mental mastery and inner stillness.

🐍
Kriya Yoga

The Path of Energy

Advanced practices working with prana, the chakra system, and kundalini energy. Integrated carefully as the practitioner matures in their practice.
