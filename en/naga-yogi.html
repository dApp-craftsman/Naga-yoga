Process

How Naga Yogi works

1

Free 1:1 consultation

We start with a conversation where we clarify how you feel, what your real goals are, and how much practice you can realistically commit to each week. No pressure, no "should" — we look for what is genuinely possible for you.

2

Your personal plan and first videos

After the consultation I build your initial structure, record a personal practice video, and select meditations and breathing techniques for you. You receive clear instructions on how and when to use each one.

3

Weekly rhythm

On your chosen practice days you work with your personal video and materials. On each practice day I send you a short resource to set the tone for the day ahead.

4

Weekly video call

Once a week we have a 30-minute video call where we:

  • Check in on how your body is responding
  • Review the physical and emotional impact of the practice
  • Correct postures, breath, and focus
  • Set the direction for the next week

This is not just a report. It is a space for honest conversation about where you are and where you want to go.

5

Progressive deepening

Each week the plan evolves with your progress — introducing more advanced techniques as your body and awareness develop.

FAQ

Common questions

Do I need yoga experience?

No. The programme can be fully adapted for complete beginners — with slower practices, more explanation, and a focus on fundamentals, breath, and safe movement.

Is it online only?

The programme can run entirely online (via video practice files and video calls). In-person sessions can be discussed depending on location.

What if I miss a session or a whole week?

Life happens — and that is a normal part of the process. In our calls we talk about what was realistic for you and adjust the plan. The focus is always on sustainable consistency, not perfection.

How long should I do the programme?

One month is a good start to feel the difference. The most meaningful shifts tend to happen over 3 or more months. We discuss what timeframe makes sense for you during the consultation.

What if I have an injury or physical limitation?

That is exactly what the personal approach is for. We discuss any injuries, chronic pain, or physical restrictions in the consultation, and everything is adapted accordingly. Nothing is forced.
